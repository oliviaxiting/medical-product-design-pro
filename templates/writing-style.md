# Medical Documentation Writing Style

## Purpose

This document defines the writing style for all AI-generated medical product design documents.

The objective is to ensure consistency, clarity, professionalism, and readability across every generated document.

This writing style applies to:

- PRDs
- Design Reviews
- Task Analysis
- Risk Analysis
- Usability Reports
- Interaction Specifications

---

## Writing Principles

Always write for professional readers.

Assume the audience includes:

- Product Managers
- UX Designers
- Engineers
- QA Engineers
- Clinical Specialists
- Regulatory Teams

---

## Tone

Use a professional and objective tone.

Avoid:

- Marketing language
- Promotional wording
- Emotional expressions
- Personal opinions

Documents should read like engineering specifications.

---

## Language

Use clear and concise language.

Prefer short sentences.

Use consistent terminology.

Avoid unnecessary repetition.

Prefer active voice whenever possible.

---

## Requirements Writing

Requirements should describe expected behavior.

Avoid implementation details.

Avoid vague expressions.

Every requirement should be independently understandable.

---

## Medical Terminology

Use standardized medical terminology whenever possible.

Avoid inventing new terms.

Use consistent naming throughout the document.

---

## Structure

Use logical hierarchy.

Group related information together.

Present information in the order users need it.

Critical information should appear before supporting details.

---

## Traceability

Whenever applicable, maintain traceability between:

Requirements

↓

Workflow

↓

Task

↓

Risk

↓

Design

↓

Validation

Every major design decision should be traceable.

---

## AI Quality Rules

Before generating any document verify:

- Is the document complete?

- Is every section necessary?

- Is every recommendation justified?

- Is patient safety considered?

- Is the document implementable?

- Is the language concise?

Only output the document after all quality checks pass.
