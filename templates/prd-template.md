# PRD Output Specification

## Purpose

This document defines the quality requirements for AI-generated Product Requirement Documents (PRDs).

The objective is to ensure every PRD is complete, traceable, implementable, testable, and aligned with medical product development practices.

This specification defines output quality rather than enforcing a fixed document structure.

---

## Target Audience

The generated PRD should be understandable by:

- Product Managers
- UX Designers
- UI Designers
- Software Engineers
- QA Engineers
- Clinical Specialists
- Regulatory Teams

---

## Output Requirements

Every PRD should:

- Clearly describe the product objective.
- Support clinical workflows.
- Be implementable by engineering teams.
- Be testable by QA teams.
- Support design review.
- Support regulatory traceability.

Do not include unnecessary content.

Only generate sections relevant to the requested product.

---

## Required Content

Depending on the project, the PRD may include:

- Product Background
- Business Objectives
- Clinical Objectives
- User Profiles
- Clinical Workflow
- Functional Requirements
- Page List
- Navigation
- Interaction Rules
- Business Rules
- Exception Handling
- Acceptance Criteria
- Constraints
- Risks
- References

AI should determine which sections are necessary.

---

## Writing Rules

Use clear and concise language.

Avoid marketing language.

Avoid ambiguous wording.

Avoid duplicated requirements.

Every requirement should be independently understandable.

Use consistent terminology throughout the document.

Prefer active voice.

Requirements should be measurable whenever possible.

---

## Requirement Writing Rules

Each requirement should contain:

- Requirement ID (optional)
- Description
- Reason
- Priority
- Related Workflow (if applicable)

Requirements should describe expected system behavior rather than implementation details.

---

## Medical Design Rules

Always ensure:

- Clinical workflow is supported.
- Patient safety is considered.
- Human factors are respected.
- Risk controls are reflected.
- Critical tasks are protected.

---

## Quality Checklist

Before generating the final PRD, verify:

□ Product objectives are clear.

□ Clinical objectives are defined.

□ Users are identified.

□ Functional requirements are complete.

□ Risks have been considered.

□ Human factors have been applied.

□ Acceptance criteria are included.

□ The document can be implemented by developers.

Only output the PRD after all checks pass.
