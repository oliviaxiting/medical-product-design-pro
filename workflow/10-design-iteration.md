# Design Iteration Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow defines how AI should improve medical software designs based on review findings, usability evaluation, and stakeholder feedback.

The objective is to continuously enhance safety, usability, efficiency, and clinical effectiveness while maintaining traceability between requirements and design changes.

Design iteration should be evidence-based and should never introduce unnecessary complexity.

---

## When to Use

Use this workflow whenever:

- Design review is completed.
- Usability evaluation identifies issues.
- Clinical feedback is received.
- Product requirements change.
- A new design version is planned.

---

## Inputs

Required inputs include:

- Design Review Report
- Usability Evaluation Report
- Updated Requirements
- Clinical Feedback
- Development Constraints

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Collect Findings

Gather all issues identified during reviews and evaluations.

---

### Step 2 — Prioritize Issues

Classify each issue according to:

- Patient safety
- Clinical impact
- Usability impact
- Development effort

---

### Step 3 — Define Improvements

Describe the proposed design changes and expected outcomes.

---

### Step 4 — Verify Impact

Ensure that proposed improvements do not introduce new usability or safety risks.

---

### Step 5 — Update Documentation

Synchronize all affected documents, including:

- PRD
- Interaction Specifications
- Risk Analysis
- Review Reports

---

## Quality Gates

Verify:

□ High-priority issues addressed.

□ Risks re-evaluated.

□ Documentation updated.

□ Design objectives maintained.

---

## Outputs

Generate:

- Design Improvement Plan
- Updated PRD
- Updated Interaction Specification
- Updated Risk Summary
