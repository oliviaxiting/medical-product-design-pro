# Design Review Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow defines how AI should perform a comprehensive review of medical software designs.

The objective is to evaluate the design from multiple perspectives, identify deficiencies, and recommend improvements before implementation.

Review should focus on usability, safety, clinical effectiveness, and regulatory compliance.

---

## When to Use

Use this workflow whenever:

- Reviewing UI designs.
- Reviewing interaction flows.
- Reviewing PRDs.
- Reviewing prototypes.
- Preparing for development.

---

## Inputs

Required inputs include:

- PRD
- Information Architecture
- Interaction Design
- Wireframes or UI Designs (if available)

Reference:

- ANSI/AAMI HE75
- IEC 62366-1
- ISO 14971

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Review Human Factors

Evaluate:

- Information hierarchy
- Readability
- Visibility
- Cognitive workload
- Accessibility

---

### Step 2 — Review Clinical Workflow

Confirm the interface supports clinical practice without unnecessary interruption.

---

### Step 3 — Review Task Completion

Verify that critical tasks can be completed efficiently and safely.

---

### Step 4 — Review Risks

Evaluate:

- Use errors
- Hazardous situations
- Risk controls
- Residual risks

---

### Step 5 — Generate Recommendations

Classify findings as:

- Critical
- Major
- Minor
- Enhancement

Provide actionable recommendations for every identified issue.

---

## Quality Gates

Verify:

□ Human factors reviewed.

□ Clinical workflow reviewed.

□ Risks reviewed.

□ Recommendations documented.

---

## Outputs

Generate:

- Design Review Report
- Risk Summary
- Improvement Recommendations
- Compliance Summary
