# Requirement Analysis Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow defines how AI should analyze product requirements before generating any design artifacts.

The objective is to transform business requirements into a structured design foundation while ensuring clinical safety, usability, and regulatory compliance.

Requirement analysis is the starting point of every medical product design activity.

---

## When to Use

Use this workflow whenever:

- A new product is proposed.
- A new feature is requested.
- An existing workflow changes.
- A new PRD needs to be generated.
- A design task begins.

This workflow must always be completed before clinical workflow analysis or interface design.

---

## Inputs

Collect the following information whenever available:

- Business objectives
- Clinical objectives
- User requirements
- Stakeholder expectations
- Product scope
- Technical constraints
- Regulatory requirements
- Existing products or references

If important information is missing, identify the gaps before continuing.

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Understand the Business Goal

Identify why the product or feature exists.

Questions to answer:

- What problem is being solved?
- Why is it important?
- What value will it create?

---

### Step 2 — Identify Clinical Objectives

Determine the intended medical purpose.

Examples:

- Monitoring
- Diagnosis
- Treatment
- Device configuration
- Clinical documentation

Clinical objectives should guide all subsequent design decisions.

---

### Step 3 — Identify Intended Users

Determine:

- Primary users
- Secondary users
- Administrators
- Service personnel

Reference the User Profile workflow when necessary.

---

### Step 4 — Identify Use Environment

Determine where the product will be used.

Examples:

- ICU
- Operating Room
- Emergency Department
- Ward
- Outpatient Clinic

Environmental constraints should be documented.

---

### Step 5 — Define Design Scope

Clearly identify:

- Included functionality
- Excluded functionality
- System boundaries
- External dependencies

Avoid expanding beyond the defined scope.

---

### Step 6 — Identify Constraints

Document all known constraints.

Examples:

- Clinical workflow
- Regulatory requirements
- Hardware limitations
- Screen size
- Performance
- Security
- Time constraints

---

### Step 7 — Define Success Criteria

Describe what constitutes a successful design.

Examples:

- Safe operation
- Efficient workflow
- Reduced cognitive workload
- Compliance with medical standards

Success criteria should be measurable whenever possible.

---

## Quality Gates

Before continuing, verify:

□ Product objectives are clear.

□ Clinical objectives are defined.

□ Intended users are identified.

□ Use environment is documented.

□ Scope is defined.

□ Constraints are documented.

□ Success criteria are established.

Do not continue if any critical information is missing.

---

## Outputs

Generate:

- Requirement Summary
- Product Objectives
- Clinical Objectives
- User Summary
- Design Scope
- Constraints
- Success Criteria

These outputs become inputs for the next workflow.

## AI Self Checklist

Before finishing, verify:

□ I understand the business problem.

□ I understand the clinical objective.

□ I know who the users are.

□ I know where the product will be used.

□ I understand the project boundaries.

□ I have identified major constraints.

□ The design objective is clearly defined.

Only after all checks pass should the workflow be considered complete.
