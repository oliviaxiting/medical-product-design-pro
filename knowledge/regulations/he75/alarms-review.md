# Alarms Review

## Purpose

This document defines review rules for alarm presentation and management based on ANSI/AAMI HE75, IEC 62366-1, and clinical human factors principles.

The objective is to improve alarm recognition, reduce alarm fatigue, and support timely clinical intervention.

This document evaluates alarm usability rather than alarm logic.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Alarms should immediately communicate abnormal conditions while minimizing unnecessary interruptions.

Alarm systems should support clinical awareness rather than increase cognitive workload.

---

## HE75 Recommendations

### Alarm Priority

Every alarm shall have a clearly defined priority.

Typical categories include:

- Critical
- High
- Medium
- Low
- Information

Priority should determine visual prominence.

---

### Visibility

Critical alarms should remain continuously visible until acknowledged or resolved.

Alarm visibility should not depend on page switching.

---

### Alarm Information

Each alarm should clearly communicate:

- Alarm title
- Severity
- Patient or device
- Time
- Current status
- Recommended action

Avoid vague descriptions.

---

### Alarm Differentiation

Different alarm priorities should be distinguishable using multiple visual channels.

Use combinations of:

- Color
- Icon
- Text
- Position

Never rely solely on color.

---

### Alarm Acknowledgement

Acknowledgement confirms user awareness.

Acknowledgement is not equivalent to resolution.

Both states should be displayed separately.

---

### Alarm Fatigue

Reduce unnecessary alarms.

Avoid:

- Duplicate alarms
- Repeated low-value alarms
- Excessive visual competition

---

## Console Application

Recommendations:

- Persistent alarm center.
- Multi-patient alarm overview.
- Alarm filtering.
- Alarm sorting.
- Quick navigation to patient details.

---

## PAD Application

Recommendations:

- Focus on current patient.
- Display only actionable alarms.
- Large acknowledgement controls.
- Clear bedside workflow.

---

## Common Risks

- Alarm fatigue.
- Hidden alarms.
- Color-only coding.
- Missing acknowledgement status.
- Alarm overload.

---

## Design Review Checklist

Before approving the design, verify:

□ Alarm priority is obvious.

□ Critical alarms remain visible.

□ Alarm information is complete.

□ Acknowledgement and resolution are different.

□ Alarm fatigue has been considered.

□ Console supports multi-patient monitoring.

□ PAD supports rapid bedside response.

---

## AI Review Rules

Always evaluate:

- Alarm visibility
- Alarm priority
- Recognition speed
- Actionability
- Alarm fatigue

Never recommend decorative alarm presentation.

Safety always has priority over aesthetics.
