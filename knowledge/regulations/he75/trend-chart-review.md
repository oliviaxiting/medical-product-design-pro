# Trend Chart Review

## Purpose

This document defines review rules for trend charts based on ANSI/AAMI HE75 human factors principles.

The objective is to improve clinical trend interpretation, reduce cognitive workload, and support evidence-based decision-making.

Trend charts should communicate change over time rather than simply display historical data.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Trend visualization should allow users to rapidly recognize patterns, detect abnormalities, compare changes, and understand clinical progression.

Charts should support clinical decisions rather than serve as decorative graphics.

---

## HE75 Recommendations

### Time Continuity

Always display a clear and continuous time axis.

Users should easily understand:

- Current time
- Historical data
- Observation duration

---

### Scale Consistency

Maintain consistent axis scaling.

Avoid changing scales between similar charts.

Never exaggerate or hide changes through inconsistent scaling.

---

### Labeling

Clearly label:

- Units
- Time
- Parameters

Avoid ambiguous abbreviations.

---

### Multiple Parameters

When displaying multiple parameters:

- Clearly distinguish each parameter.
- Avoid excessive overlap.
- Maintain readability.

---

### Event Markers

Display clinically significant events such as:

- Medication
- Procedure
- Alarm occurrence
- Device adjustment

Events should provide context for interpreting trends.

---

### Visual Simplicity

Avoid:

- Decorative effects
- Excessive gridlines
- 3D graphics
- Unnecessary colors

Charts should emphasize data.

---

## Console Application

Recommendations:

- Support long-term monitoring.
- Display multiple synchronized trends.
- Enable comparison across parameters.
- Support zoom without losing context.

---

## PAD Application

Recommendations:

- Display essential trends only.
- Simplify interaction.
- Highlight recent changes.
- Reduce chart complexity.

---

## Common Risks

- Inconsistent scaling.
- Overlapping curves.
- Missing units.
- Excessive visual clutter.
- Hidden abnormal trends.

---

## Design Review Checklist

Before approving the design, verify:

□ Time axis is clear.

□ Units are displayed.

□ Scaling is consistent.

□ Important events are marked.

□ Charts support rapid interpretation.

□ Console supports comparison.

□ PAD supports quick review.

---

## AI Review Rules

Always evaluate:

- Trend readability
- Scale consistency
- Clinical usefulness
- Comparison efficiency

Never distort data for visual appearance.
