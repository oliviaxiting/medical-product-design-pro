# Spacing Review

## Purpose

This document defines review rules for spacing and visual grouping based on ANSI/AAMI HE75 human factors principles.

The objective is to improve visual organization, reduce cognitive workload, and support rapid information recognition.

This document reviews spacing quality rather than defining a visual style.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Spacing should communicate relationships between information and actions.

Users should understand which elements belong together without relying on borders or decorative graphics.

---

## HE75 Recommendations

### Visual Grouping

Related information should be positioned closer together than unrelated information.

Spacing should reinforce information hierarchy.

---

### White Space

Provide sufficient white space to improve readability and reduce visual clutter.

Avoid filling every available area.

---

### Consistency

Maintain consistent spacing across similar components and pages.

Avoid arbitrary spacing.

---

### Separation

Separate different functional areas clearly.

Avoid visual ambiguity between independent groups.

---

### Interaction Spacing

Maintain adequate spacing between adjacent interactive controls.

Reduce accidental activation.

---

## Console Application

Recommendations:

- Support high information density while maintaining readability.
- Keep spacing consistent across dashboards.
- Use spacing to reinforce information hierarchy.

---

## PAD Application

Recommendations:

- Increase spacing around touch controls.
- Improve readability during bedside operation.
- Avoid crowded layouts.

---

## Common Risks

- Crowded interfaces.
- Inconsistent spacing.
- Weak visual grouping.
- Difficult scanning.
- Accidental touch.

---

## Design Review Checklist

Before approving the design, verify:

□ Related information is visually grouped.

□ White space improves readability.

□ Spacing is consistent.

□ Interactive controls are sufficiently separated.

□ Console supports prolonged monitoring.

□ PAD supports reliable touch interaction.

---

## AI Review Rules

Always evaluate:

- Visual grouping
- Readability
- Cognitive workload
- Touch safety

Never recommend reducing spacing solely to display more information.
