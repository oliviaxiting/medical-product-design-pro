# Touch Target Review

## Purpose

This document defines touch interaction review rules based on ANSI/AAMI HE75 and IEC 62366-1.

The objective is to improve touch accuracy, reduce accidental activation, and support safe bedside operation.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Touch interactions should be reliable, efficient, and tolerant of human variability.

Interfaces should support users wearing gloves, working under stress, and operating in time-critical situations.

---

## HE75 Recommendations

### Touch Target Size

Touch targets should be large enough for reliable activation.

Avoid small controls that require precise finger placement.

Target size should be determined according to device size, viewing distance, and clinical use scenario.

---

### Spacing

Maintain sufficient spacing between adjacent controls.

Prevent accidental activation.

---

### Frequently Used Controls

Place frequently used controls within comfortable reach.

Avoid requiring unnecessary hand movement.

---

### Critical Operations

Separate critical actions from routine actions.

Reduce the possibility of accidental activation.

Provide confirmation only when appropriate.

---

### Gesture Usage

Avoid complex gestures.

Prefer direct manipulation.

Interactions should remain predictable.

---

## Console Application

Recommendations:

- Mouse and keyboard remain primary input.
- Touch optimization is optional unless required by hardware.
- Prevent accidental clicks on dense interfaces.

---

## PAD Application

Recommendations:

- Optimize for finger operation.
- Support operation while wearing medical gloves.
- Increase spacing between controls.
- Prioritize one-handed operation when appropriate.

---

## Common Risks

- Touch targets too small.
- Controls too close together.
- Accidental activation.
- Overuse of gestures.
- Hidden interactions.

---

## Design Review Checklist

Before approving the design, verify:

□ Touch targets are easy to activate.

□ Adjacent controls are sufficiently separated.

□ Critical actions are protected.

□ Frequently used controls are easy to reach.

□ PAD supports reliable bedside operation.

---

## AI Review Rules

Always evaluate:

- Touch accuracy
- Reachability
- Accidental activation risk
- Clinical workflow efficiency

Never recommend reducing touch target size simply to increase information density.
