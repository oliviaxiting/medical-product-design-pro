# Information Density Review

## Purpose

This document defines review rules for information density based on ANSI/AAMI HE75 human factors principles.

The objective is to balance information completeness with cognitive workload.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Medical interfaces should display sufficient information for safe decision-making without overwhelming users.

Information density should always match the clinical context.

---

## HE75 Recommendations

### Clinical Priority

Display information according to clinical importance.

Avoid presenting all information with equal emphasis.

---

### Progressive Disclosure

Show essential information first.

Reveal additional information only when needed.

---

### Visual Hierarchy

Use typography, spacing, and layout to guide attention.

Avoid uniform visual weight.

---

### Scanning

Users should identify key information within a few seconds.

Support rapid visual scanning.

---

### Cognitive Load

Reduce unnecessary:

- Text
- Icons
- Colors
- Charts
- Decorative elements

Every element should have clinical value.

---

### Information Balance

Too little information may delay decisions.

Too much information increases cognitive workload.

Maintain an appropriate balance.

---

## Console Application

Recommendations:

- Higher information density is acceptable.
- Preserve readability.
- Support filtering.
- Support customization.

---

## PAD Application

Recommendations:

- Display only task-relevant information.
- Reduce secondary information.
- Minimize scrolling.
- Focus on current workflow.

---

## Common Risks

- Information overload.
- Visual clutter.
- Hidden critical information.
- Excessive scrolling.
- Poor prioritization.

---

## Design Review Checklist

Before approving the design, verify:

□ Information priority is clear.

□ Visual hierarchy is effective.

□ Important information is easy to find.

□ Secondary information is appropriately reduced.

□ Console supports efficient monitoring.

□ PAD supports focused bedside interaction.

---

## AI Review Rules

Always evaluate:

- Cognitive workload
- Information hierarchy
- Clinical relevance
- Scan efficiency

Never recommend increasing information density at the expense of readability.
