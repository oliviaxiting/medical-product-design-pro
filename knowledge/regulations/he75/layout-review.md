# Layout Review

## Purpose

This document defines layout review rules based on ANSI/AAMI HE75 human factors principles.

The objective is to organize information in a way that supports rapid perception, efficient navigation, and safe clinical decision-making.

This document is intended for design review rather than defining a visual style.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Layout should help users locate, interpret, and act on information with minimal cognitive effort.

Information placement should reflect clinical priority and workflow.

---

## HE75 Recommendations

### Information Hierarchy

Organize information by clinical importance rather than visual aesthetics.

The most important information should appear in the most prominent locations.

---

### Grouping

Group related information together.

Separate unrelated information using spacing rather than decorative borders whenever possible.

---

### Alignment

Maintain consistent alignment throughout the interface.

Avoid randomly positioned elements.

---

### White Space

Use sufficient white space to improve readability and visual grouping.

Avoid overcrowded layouts.

---

### Consistency

Maintain consistent page structure across similar screens.

Users should not relearn layouts between workflows.

---

### Visual Balance

Avoid placing too many important elements in one area.

Distribute visual attention appropriately.

---

## Console Application

Recommendations:

- Support high information density while maintaining readability.
- Keep navigation stable.
- Place monitoring information in predictable locations.
- Reduce unnecessary scrolling.

---

## PAD Application

Recommendations:

- Prioritize the current patient and active task.
- Keep layouts simple.
- Minimize interaction depth.
- Reduce scrolling whenever possible.

---

## Common Risks

- Information overload.
- Poor grouping.
- Excessive scrolling.
- Inconsistent layouts.
- Important information placed in secondary locations.

---

## Design Review Checklist

Before approving the design, verify:

□ Information hierarchy is clear.

□ Related information is grouped.

□ Alignment is consistent.

□ White space supports readability.

□ Layout is consistent across pages.

□ Console supports efficient monitoring.

□ PAD supports bedside workflows.

---

## AI Review Rules

Always evaluate:

- Information hierarchy
- Grouping
- Navigation efficiency
- Reading flow
- Workflow continuity

Never recommend decorative layouts that reduce usability.
