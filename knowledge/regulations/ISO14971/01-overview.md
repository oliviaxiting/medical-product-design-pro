# ISO 14971 Overview

## Purpose

This document introduces ISO 14971 and explains how risk management supports the development of safe medical devices.

Unlike interface design guidelines, ISO 14971 focuses on identifying, evaluating, controlling, and monitoring risks throughout the medical device lifecycle.

This knowledge is intended to guide AI reasoning when making product, interaction, and interface design decisions.

---

## What is ISO 14971

ISO 14971 is the international standard for risk management of medical devices.

The purpose of risk management is not to eliminate all risks.

The objective is to reduce risks to an acceptable level while maintaining the intended medical benefit.

Risk management applies throughout the entire product lifecycle.

---

## Relationship with Other Standards

ISO 14971 works together with other medical standards.

| Standard | Primary Focus |
|-----------|---------------|
| ANSI/AAMI HE75 | Human Factors Design |
| IEC 62366-1 | Usability Engineering |
| ISO 14971 | Risk Management |
| FDA Human Factors Guidance | Regulatory Validation |

HE75 improves interface quality.

IEC 62366 reduces use errors.

ISO 14971 evaluates whether those errors could cause patient harm.

These standards should always be considered together.

---

## Core Philosophy

Every design decision introduces risk.

Risk management does not begin after development.

Risk management begins when product requirements are defined.

Every feature should be evaluated from a patient safety perspective.

---

## Core Concepts

ISO 14971 introduces the following concepts:

- Hazard
- Hazardous Situation
- Harm
- Risk
- Risk Analysis
- Risk Evaluation
- Risk Control
- Residual Risk
- Benefit-Risk Analysis

These concepts guide safe product development.

---

## Application to Medical Software

Risk management applies equally to:

- Console applications
- PAD applications
- Embedded software
- Cloud platforms
- Connected medical systems

Software interfaces are part of the medical device and can contribute to clinical risk.

---

## AI Thinking Rules

Before approving any design, AI should ask:

What hazards exist?

Could this interface contribute to patient harm?

How can the design reduce risk?

Is the remaining risk acceptable?

Safety has higher priority than convenience.
