# Risk Evaluation

## Purpose

This document defines how analyzed risks should be evaluated according to ISO 14971.

The objective is to determine whether identified risks are acceptable or require additional control measures.

Risk evaluation supports design decisions and prioritizes mitigation efforts.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Not all risks require the same level of action.

Risk evaluation compares the estimated risk against predefined acceptability criteria.

Only acceptable risks may remain without further control.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Each identified risk should be assessed based on:

- Severity
- Probability
- Clinical impact
- Patient safety
- Intended medical benefit

Evaluation should be consistent across the product.

---

## Risk Prioritization

Higher priority should be given to risks that:

- Could result in patient harm
- Affect critical clinical tasks
- Are difficult for users to detect
- Occur during high-stress situations
- Influence treatment decisions

---

## Decision Outcomes

After evaluation, each risk should be classified as:

- Acceptable
- Acceptable with monitoring
- Requires additional control
- Unacceptable

Document the rationale for every decision.

---

## Console Application

Evaluate whether:

- Alarm-related risks are acceptable.
- Information density affects safe monitoring.
- Navigation supports timely decision-making.

---

## PAD Application

Evaluate whether:

- Touch interaction is reliable.
- Bedside workflows reduce errors.
- Confirmation steps are appropriate.

---

## AI Thinking Rules

For every analyzed risk, determine:

- Is this risk acceptable?
- If not, what additional control is required?
- Which design changes would most effectively reduce the risk?

Never accept a risk without documented justification.
