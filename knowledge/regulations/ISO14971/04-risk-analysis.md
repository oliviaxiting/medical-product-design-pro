# Risk Analysis

## Purpose

This document defines how to analyze identified risks according to ISO 14971.

The objective is to understand the relationship between hazards, hazardous situations, foreseeable sequences of events, and potential harm.

Risk analysis should be performed before selecting risk control measures.

---

## Human Factors Principle

A hazard alone does not represent risk.

Risk exists when a hazard can reasonably lead to harm through a foreseeable sequence of events.

Risk analysis should focus on understanding how harm could occur.

---

## Risk Analysis Process

For each identified hazard, analyze:

Hazard

↓

Foreseeable Sequence of Events

↓

Hazardous Situation

↓

Potential Harm

↓

Severity

↓

Probability

---

## Foreseeable Sequence of Events

Identify realistic events that could lead to harm.

Examples:

- Incorrect user action
- Delayed response
- Misinterpreted information
- Software malfunction
- Device communication failure

Avoid unrealistic assumptions.

---

## Severity Assessment

Evaluate the potential impact if harm occurs.

Examples:

- No injury
- Minor injury
- Serious injury
- Permanent impairment
- Death

Severity should reflect clinical consequences.

---

## Probability Assessment

Estimate how likely the hazardous situation is to occur.

Consider:

- Frequency of use
- User experience
- Environmental conditions
- Interface complexity
- Historical evidence

Probability should be based on objective reasoning whenever possible.

---

## Console Application

Typical analysis focuses on:

- Alarm management
- Multi-patient monitoring
- Trend interpretation
- Device configuration

---

## PAD Application

Typical analysis focuses on:

- Touch interaction
- Patient confirmation
- Parameter adjustment
- Bedside workflow

---

## AI Thinking Rules

For every identified hazard, determine:

- What sequence of events leads to harm?
- How severe could the outcome be?
- How likely is it to occur?
- Which design factors contribute to the risk?

Do not evaluate risks without identifying the complete event sequence.
