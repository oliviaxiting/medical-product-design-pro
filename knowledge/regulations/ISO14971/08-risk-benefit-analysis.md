# Risk-Benefit Analysis

## Purpose

This document defines how risk-benefit analysis should be performed according to ISO 14971.

The objective is to determine whether the expected medical benefits justify any remaining residual risks.

Risk-benefit analysis should be performed when residual risks cannot be further reduced by practical design measures.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Some medical treatments involve unavoidable risks.

A medical device may still be considered acceptable if its clinical benefits clearly outweigh the remaining risks.

Risk-benefit decisions should always prioritize patient safety while considering therapeutic value.

---

## Risk-Benefit Process

The analysis should include:

Medical Benefit

↓

Residual Risk

↓

Clinical Context

↓

Alternative Solutions

↓

Overall Assessment

↓

Design Decision

Risk-benefit analysis should be evidence-based whenever possible.

---

## Medical Benefits

Potential benefits may include:

- Earlier diagnosis
- Improved treatment accuracy
- Faster clinical intervention
- Reduced clinician workload
- Improved patient outcomes
- Increased operational efficiency

Benefits should be relevant to the intended medical purpose.

---

## Residual Risks

Residual risks may include:

- Remaining use errors
- Workflow delays
- Alarm fatigue
- Misinterpretation
- Accidental operation

Residual risks should be minimized before benefit analysis is performed.

---

## Alternative Solutions

Consider whether:

- A safer design is available.
- The workflow can be simplified.
- Additional interface improvements are feasible.
- Different interaction methods could further reduce risk.

Benefit analysis should not replace good design.

---

## Console Application

Typical considerations include:

- Faster monitoring
- Improved situational awareness
- Efficient management of multiple patients
- Reduced configuration errors

Evaluate whether these benefits outweigh any remaining usability risks.

---

## PAD Application

Typical considerations include:

- Faster bedside interaction
- Reduced documentation effort
- Improved touch efficiency
- Rapid clinical confirmation

Evaluate whether these benefits justify any unavoidable residual risks.

---

## AI Thinking Rules

Before approving a design, AI should ask:

What clinical benefit does this feature provide?

What residual risks remain?

Can the same benefit be achieved with lower risk?

Does the clinical value justify the remaining risk?

Never justify unnecessary complexity by claiming increased functionality.

Patient benefit must always outweigh residual risk.
