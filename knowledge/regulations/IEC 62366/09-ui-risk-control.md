# UI Risk Control

## Purpose

This document defines interface-based risk control measures according to IEC 62366-1.

The objective is to reduce use-related risks through interface design rather than relying solely on user training.

---

## Human Factors Principle

Whenever possible, risk should be reduced by improving the interface.

Training should not be the primary risk control.

Users should naturally perform the correct action.

---

## Typical UI Risk Controls

Risk controls include:

- Clear information hierarchy
- Consistent navigation
- Obvious system status
- Immediate feedback
- Error prevention
- Confirmation for critical actions
- Visual emphasis
- Progressive disclosure
- Context awareness

---

## Confirmation

Confirmation should only be used for:

- Irreversible actions
- High-risk operations
- Patient safety events

Avoid unnecessary confirmation dialogs.

---

## Error Prevention

Prevent incorrect actions before they occur.

Examples:

- Disable unavailable actions
- Validate input immediately
- Prevent impossible operations
- Highlight critical information

---

## Error Recovery

Support recovery by:

- Undo where appropriate
- Clear recovery guidance
- Meaningful error messages
- Preserve user progress

---

## Console Application

Focus on:

- Monitoring continuity
- Multi-patient awareness
- Alarm visibility
- Safe configuration

---

## PAD Application

Focus on:

- Touch accuracy
- Simple workflows
- Bedside efficiency
- Rapid confirmation

---

## AI Thinking Rules

For every identified risk, AI should recommend at least one interface-based mitigation.

Prefer prevention over detection.

Prefer detection over recovery.

Training should be the last option.
