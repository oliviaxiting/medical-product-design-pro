# Usability Validation

## Purpose

This document defines usability validation principles according to IEC 62366-1.

The objective is to demonstrate that intended users can safely and effectively complete critical clinical tasks using the designed interface.

Validation should evaluate real-world performance rather than subjective preference.

---

## Human Factors Principle

A design is considered usable only when representative users successfully complete representative tasks under representative conditions.

Expert review alone is not sufficient.

---

## Validation Objectives

Validation should verify that:

- Critical tasks can be completed successfully.
- Use errors are minimized.
- Users understand system feedback.
- Patient safety is maintained.
- Clinical workflow is supported.

---

## Validation Participants

Representative users may include:

- Nurses
- Physicians
- Biomedical engineers
- Clinical technicians

Participants should reflect actual product users.

---

## Validation Environment

Validation should resemble the intended clinical environment.

Consider:

- Noise
- Lighting
- Interruptions
- Time pressure
- Clinical workload

---

## Critical Tasks

Validation should prioritize:

- Alarm acknowledgement
- Patient identification
- Parameter adjustment
- Device configuration
- Clinical documentation

---

## Success Criteria

Typical measures include:

- Task completion rate
- Task completion time
- Number of use errors
- Number of close calls
- User understanding
- Recovery ability

---

## AI Thinking Rules

Whenever a design is completed, AI should ask:

Can representative users safely complete every critical task?

If not,

What should be redesigned?

Validation should always focus on patient safety rather than visual quality.
