# IEC 62366-1 Overview

## Purpose

This document introduces IEC 62366-1 and explains how usability engineering contributes to the safe design of medical devices.

Unlike traditional interface design guidelines, IEC 62366 focuses on preventing use-related hazards caused by human interaction with a medical device.

This knowledge is intended to guide AI reasoning during product definition, interaction design, usability review, and PRD generation.

---

## What is IEC 62366-1

IEC 62366-1 is the international standard for applying usability engineering throughout the medical device development lifecycle.

The objective is not simply to create an interface that is easy to use.

The objective is to reduce use-related risk and improve patient safety.

Usability engineering is therefore considered part of the overall medical device risk management process.

---

## Relationship with Other Standards

IEC 62366 complements other medical design standards.

| Standard | Primary Focus |
|-----------|---------------|
| ANSI/AAMI HE75 | Human factors design guidance |
| IEC 62366-1 | Usability engineering process |
| ISO 14971 | Risk management |
| ISO 9241 | Ergonomics of human-system interaction |
| FDA Human Factors Guidance | Regulatory expectations for usability validation |

HE75 explains how interfaces should be designed.

IEC 62366 explains how usability engineering should be performed.

ISO 14971 explains how risks should be identified, evaluated, and controlled.

These standards should be applied together rather than independently.

---

## Core Philosophy

A visually attractive interface is not necessarily a safe interface.

Usability exists to reduce the probability of use errors.

Every interaction should support safe, effective, and efficient clinical operation.

Patient safety always has higher priority than visual aesthetics.

---

## Core Concepts

IEC 62366 introduces the following concepts:

- Intended Use
- Intended User
- Use Specification
- User Interface
- Use Scenario
- Task Analysis
- Use Error
- Hazard-Related Use Scenario
- Risk Control
- Usability Validation

These concepts form the usability engineering workflow.

---

## Application to Console Products

Typical Console workflows include:

- Multi-patient monitoring
- Alarm management
- Device configuration
- Historical data review
- Clinical documentation

Design should support prolonged operation with high information density.

---

## Application to PAD Products

Typical PAD workflows include:

- Bedside monitoring
- Patient review
- Device adjustment
- Clinical confirmation
- Rapid intervention

Design should emphasize touch interaction, simplicity, and rapid task completion.

---

## AI Thinking Rules

Before generating any interface, always determine:

- Who is the intended user?
- What clinical task is being performed?
- Where will the product be used?
- What risks exist?
- How can the interface reduce those risks?

Never begin interface design before establishing the usability context.
