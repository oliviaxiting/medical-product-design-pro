# Use Scenario

## Purpose

This document defines how to analyze clinical use scenarios according to IEC 62366-1.

The objective is to understand the context in which the interface will be used so that interaction design reflects real clinical environments.

---

## Human Factors Principle

The same task performed in different environments may require different interface designs.

Environmental conditions directly influence usability and safety.

---

## Scenario Definition

Each use scenario should describe:

- Clinical location
- User
- Device
- Patient
- Objective
- Workflow
- Time pressure
- Environmental constraints

---

## Typical Clinical Scenarios

### ICU

Characteristics:

- Continuous monitoring
- Multiple alarms
- High patient acuity
- Frequent interruptions

Design priorities:

- Situational awareness
- Alarm visibility
- Rapid navigation

---

### Operating Room

Characteristics:

- Sterile environment
- High concentration
- Time-critical decisions

Design priorities:

- Minimal interaction
- Large controls
- Clear status visibility

---

### General Ward

Characteristics:

- Periodic review
- Lower urgency
- Multiple patients

Design priorities:

- Efficient navigation
- Simplified workflows

---

### Emergency Department

Characteristics:

- High stress
- Frequent interruptions
- Unpredictable workflow

Design priorities:

- Rapid recognition
- Minimal cognitive load
- Fast task completion

---

## Console Application

Typical scenarios:

- Central monitoring
- Device management
- Clinical review
- Historical analysis

---

## PAD Application

Typical scenarios:

- Bedside review
- Patient confirmation
- Device adjustment
- Clinical intervention

---

## AI Thinking Rules

Always identify:

- Where is the user?
- What is happening?
- What interruptions may occur?
- What information is immediately required?

Never design outside the intended clinical scenario.
